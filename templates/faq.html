Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

General Novated Lease Questions

What is a novated lease? ▼

A novated lease is a three-way agreement between you (the employee), your employer, and the lease company. Your employer pays the lease and vehicle running costs directly from your pre-tax salary, which can reduce your taxable income.

How does a novated lease work? ▼

Your employer deducts funds from your pre-tax (and sometimes post-tax) salary to cover your car repayments and agreed-upon running costs (like fuel, insurance, servicing). These deductions are then paid to the lease provider. This 'salary sacrifice' can reduce your taxable income, potentially leading to tax savings.

What are the key benefits of a novated lease? ▼

Benefits often include potential income tax savings due to pre-tax deductions, GST savings on the car's purchase price (for your employer) and running costs, and convenience as your car expenses are bundled into one easy payment.

Are there any disadvantages or risks to a novated lease? ▼

Potential downsides include Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT) considerations (though often managed), early termination fees if you leave your employer or the lease early, and being tied into a lease agreement. It's important to understand the full terms.

Who is eligible for a novated lease? ▼

Eligibility typically depends on your employer offering novated leasing as part of their salary packaging options. You generally need to be a permanent employee with a sufficient salary to cover the deductions.

Vehicle & Lease Specifics

What types of vehicles can be novated? ▼

Most new and used vehicles (up to a certain age) can be novated. Electric Vehicles (EVs) and Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs) often have additional tax benefits making them particularly attractive for novated leasing.

What happens at the end of a novated lease term? ▼

At the end of the lease, you typically have a few options: pay the 'residual value' to own the car outright, re-lease the vehicle for another term, or trade it in/sell it to pay off the residual.

*
What is a "residual value" and how is it calculated? ▼

The residual value is the estimated value of the car at the end of the lease term, which you are required to pay to take ownership. It's set by the ATO and depends on the lease term and initial vehicle value.

Can I include all my running costs (fuel, insurance, service) in the lease? ▼

Yes, one of the major benefits of a novated lease is the ability to bundle most of your car's running costs into your pre-tax salary deductions, including fuel/charging, maintenance, tyres, registration, and insurance.

Financial & Tax Considerations (Australia)

How does Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT) apply to novated leases? ▼

FBT can apply to the private use of a novated lease vehicle. However, it's often offset by contributions from your post-tax salary, effectively reducing or eliminating the FBT liability for you and your employer. EV/PHEV vehicles often have FBT exemptions.

How can Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT) impact negatively on pre-tax savings? ▼

Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT) is a tax paid by employers on certain benefits provided to their employees, which are not salary or wages. When you use a novated lease, your employer is providing you with a "car fringe benefit" because the lease payments and often running costs are deducted from your pre-tax salary.

Technically, your employer is liable for FBT. However, in most novated lease arrangements, this cost is structured to be passed on to the employee through salary deductions. This means that while you gain the benefit of reducing your taxable income through pre-tax payments, a portion of your benefit is subject to FBT. If FBT is not managed, it can offset some of the income tax savings you achieve by salary packaging. For instance, if you pay for everything from pre-tax income and an FBT liability arises, that liability ultimately affects your overall financial benefit from the lease. The FBT rate is currently 47% (equivalent to the highest marginal tax rate plus the Medicare levy), applied to the taxable value of the car benefit.

To avoid or reduce FBT, the most common method is the **Employee Contribution Method (ECM)**. This involves making a portion of your novated lease payments (specifically, an amount equal to the FBT taxable value) from your **after-tax** salary. Every dollar contributed post-tax reduces the FBT liability by the same amount, potentially bringing the FBT to zero. While this means some of your payments are post-tax, for many, the combined tax savings still outweigh paying for the car outright.

Since July 1, 2022, eligible Electric Vehicles (BEVs, FCEVs, and certain PHEVs until March 31, 2025) under a novated lease are exempt from FBT, provided the car's value is below the luxury car tax threshold. This exemption significantly enhances pre-tax savings as it eliminates the need for post-tax contributions to offset FBT for these vehicles.

How do GST savings work with a novated lease? ▼

When your employer purchases the vehicle (and pays for running costs) through the lease, they can claim back the GST. This saving is typically passed on to you, reducing the effective price of the car and your running expenses.

How does a novated lease affect my take-home pay? ▼

While deductions are made from your gross salary, the tax savings (income tax and GST) often mean your overall financial position is improved, and your net take-home pay might be higher than if you financed a car personally with after-tax money.

Calculator Specifics

What calculations are used within the form and how are results derived? ▼

Your calculator performs comprehensive financial comparisons by taking into account various inputs and applying Australian tax and financial guidelines. Key calculations often include:

  • Novated Lease Financials:
    • Lease Payments: Calculated based on the vehicle price, novated lease interest rate, and the lease term.
    • Residual Value (Balloon Payment): This is the projected value of the car at the end of the lease term, which you must pay if you wish to own the car. The Australian Taxation Office (ATO) sets minimum residual value percentages based on the lease term to ensure compliance (e.g., typically 46.88% for a 3-year lease).
    • Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT) Taxable Value:
      • Statutory Formula Method (Most Common): Calculates FBT based on a fixed percentage (currently 20%) of the car's base value (drive-away price less government on-road costs), regardless of kilometers travelled.
      • Operating Cost Method (Less Common for personal leases): Calculates FBT based on the actual operating costs and the private use percentage of the vehicle. This method requires maintaining a logbook.
    • Estimated Annual Income Tax Saving: This is calculated by comparing your tax liability with your gross annual salary versus your tax liability after pre-tax salary deductions for the novated lease and packaged running costs.
    • GST Savings: Calculates the 10% Goods and Services Tax saved on the vehicle purchase price (as the employer claims the GST credit) and on included running costs.
  • Car Loan Financials:
    • Annual Repayments & Total Interest Paid: Calculated based on the vehicle price, car loan interest rate, and loan term.
  • Overall Cost Comparison: The calculator compares the "Total Net Cost Over Term" and "Effective Monthly/Annual Cost" for both the Novated Lease and Car Loan scenarios, taking into account all payments, tax savings, GST savings, and FBT implications (offset by ECM where applicable).
What data is used to base the calculator's figures on? ▼

The accuracy of the calculations depends heavily on the input data you provide and certain underlying assumptions. Your calculator uses the following key data points:

  • Your Personal Financials:
    • Gross Annual Salary (AUD): Used to determine your marginal tax rate and calculate income tax savings.
    • Private Health Cover (Y/N): Relevant for Medicare Levy Surcharge calculations.
  • Vehicle & Lease Details:
    • Vehicle Price (AUD): The drive-away price of the vehicle, including GST and stamp duty, which forms the basis for lease and loan calculations.
    • Lease/Loan Term (Years): The duration of the finance agreement (e.g., 1, 3, 5 years).
    • Annual Kilometers: Your estimated annual driving distance, which influences the budgeted running costs (fuel, tyres, servicing).
    • Is Electric Vehicle (EV/PHEV) for FBT exemption? (Y/N): Crucial for determining if FBT exemption applies, significantly impacting overall savings.
  • Interest Rates:
    • Novated Lease Interest Rate (% P.A.): The interest rate applied to the novated lease finance.
    • Car Loan Interest Rate (% P.A.): The interest rate for a traditional car loan comparison.
  • Estimated Annual Running Costs (Budgeted):
    • Fuel/Charging (AUD): Your estimated annual cost for fuel or electricity.
    • Service & Tyres (AUD): Estimated annual costs for maintenance and tyre replacement.
    • Insurance (AUD): Estimated annual premium for comprehensive car insurance.
    • Registration (AUD): Estimated annual vehicle registration fee.

Underlying Assumptions:
Calculators typically make certain assumptions based on ATO guidelines and general market rates. While your calculator provides estimates, for precise figures, it's always recommended to obtain a personalized quote from a novated lease provider who can factor in specific deals, current rates, and your individual circumstances.

Comparison & Decision Making

How does a novated lease compare to a car loan or outright purchase? ▼

A novated lease leverages pre-tax salary deductions and GST savings for potential tax efficiencies. A car loan uses after-tax income with interest, and outright purchase requires significant upfront capital. Our calculator can help you compare these options! Calculate now.

Is a novated lease right for me? ▼

It depends on your individual financial situation, annual salary, employer's willingness to offer novated leasing, and your driving habits. It's generally most beneficial for those looking to maximize tax efficiencies on their vehicle expenses.
